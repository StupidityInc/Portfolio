How I Got Here

I started programming in Python at a young age — not because someone told me to, but because I wanted to understand how things worked. Breaking stuff and figuring out how to fix it became a habit, then an obsession.

At 14, I joined the Odyssia program, an accelerated track for CS and Mathematics at Tel Aviv University. It wasn't just about moving faster; it was about being around people who take ideas seriously. That's where raw curiosity turned into disciplined thinking.

I'm not just collecting languages or frameworks. I'm learning how to build things that actually hold up.
